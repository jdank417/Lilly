About Dr. Hanley

Dr. Lilly Hanley is a pioneering biotechnologist specializing in yeast cellular biology and intercellular transport mechanisms. With over a decade of research experience, she has made significant contributions to our understanding of how cellular components move within and between yeast cells.

Her innovative approaches combine advanced microscopy techniques with genetic engineering to visualize and manipulate transport processes in real-time, providing unprecedented insights into these fundamental biological mechanisms.

Research Focus

Vesicular Transport

Investigating how vesicles package and transport proteins between organelles in yeast cells.

Membrane Dynamics

Studying the fluidity and selective permeability of yeast cell membranes.

Protein Trafficking

Tracking the movement of specific proteins through the secretory pathway.

Organelle Communication

Exploring how different cellular compartments coordinate their activities.
